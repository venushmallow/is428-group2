Problem Statement & Motivation

There has been a tremendous increase in the global incidence of dengue, with approximately 50 percent of the worldâ€™s population at risk. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the number of reported dengue cases has increased rapidly over the past 20 years, from 505,430 cases in 2000 to 5.2 million cases in 2019. The number of reported deaths from dengue has risen from 960 to 4032 between 2000 and 2015, with the younger demographics being affected the most.

To facilitate the analysis of the dengue problem, the team will be designing and implementing a visualisation platform, which provides users with a better understanding of the dengue problem both globally and locally (in Singapore), insights on how various factors have contributed towards worsening or improving the dengue problem as well as predictions of future dengue outbreaks. The factors included in the analysis are location, the effectiveness of current efforts such as Project Wolbachia-Singapore, climatic conditions, et cetera. Authorities or organisations can also consider these insights when deciding on expanding current efforts and implementing new measures.
